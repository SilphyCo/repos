Mum's Special Katsu

Back to Home

Prep Time

20 mins

Cook Time

30 mins

Servings

4 people

Difficulty

Medium

A delicious & classic plate waiting to be dished to your loved ones

This comforting Japanese favorite uses fresh ingredients and simple techniques to create a crispy breaded cutlet served with a rich, flavorful curry sauce—no artificial additives, just wholesome goodness that will keep hunger at bay.

For the Katsu

  • Chicken breasts or pork cutlets (boneless, pounded thin)
  • Salt and pepper
  • All-purpose flour
  • Eggs (beaten)
  • Panko breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

For the Curry

  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • Carrot (diced)
  • Potatoes (peeled and diced)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Ginger (grated)
  • Japanese curry roux blocks
  • Chicken or vegetable broth

For Serving

  • Cooked rice
  • Pickled ginger (optional)
  • Green onions (chopped)
  • White sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Prepare the cutlets: Season the chicken or pork with salt and pepper. Set up a breading station with three shallow dishes: flour, beaten eggs, and panko breadcrumbs.
  2. Bread the cutlets: Dredge each cutlet in flour, shaking off excess. Dip in the egg, then coat evenly with panko breadcrumbs. Set aside.
  3. Fry the cutlets: Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan over medium heat. When hot, fry the breaded cutlets for 3–4 minutes per side until golden and cooked through. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. Make the curry sauce: In a separate pot, heat a bit of oil over medium heat. Add chopped onion, carrot, and potatoes. Cook for 5–7 minutes until vegetables soften. Add garlic and ginger, cooking 1 more minute.
  5. Add broth and simmer: Pour in the broth, enough to cover the vegetables. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes, until vegetables are tender.
  6. Incorporate curry roux: Break the curry roux blocks into the pot and stir until fully dissolved. Simmer gently until sauce thickens, about 5 more minutes. Adjust seasoning as needed.
  7. Serve: Slice the fried cutlets. Serve over a bed of steamed rice, topped generously with the curry sauce. Enjoy hot!

Pro Tips

  • Pound meat evenly for consistent cooking
  • Use fresh panko for extra crispiness
  • Let oil reach 350°F before frying

Variations

  • Try with tofu for vegetarian option
  • Add apples for sweeter curry
  • Use turkey or beef cutlets
